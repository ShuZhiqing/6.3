India

Average daily Indian nutrition intake has high amount of protein, phosphorus, vitamin B6 and C. However, the foods are lack of vitamin A, B12, and calcium because many people from India are vegetarian; meanwhile the main source of vitamin A and B12 are from animal products. If you are not eligible for Medicare, by law you are required to have private health insurance while you are living in Australia.

Food source rich in:
  • Calcium: milk, buttermilk, beans, lentils
  • Vitamin A: butter, cheese, egg, sweet potato, squash, carrot
  • Vitamin B6: fish, beef liver and other organ meats, potatoes and other starchy vegetables, and fruit (other than citrus)
  • Vitamin B12: milk, egg

Indians food preferences vary regionally. North Indian people prefer roti, parathas, and a vegetable dish accompanied by pickle and some curd with tea or coffee. People of western India prefer dhokla and milk; while south Indians prefer idli and dosa. Traditional lunch in India usually consists of a main dish of rice or whole wheat roti and typically includes vegetables. Apart from that, many households prefer having sweets after the dinner, similar like the western concept of dessert after meals. The families often gather for evening snack time, similar to tea time to talk and have tea and snacks.

Rice and flatbreads are the most contributing calories in Indian food. However, either roti, idli, dosa, dhokla, paratha, and rice almost have similar amount of nutritional content. Their protein contents are in the range of 4% to 13% per serving where paratha and roti made of flour have the highest while idli has the lowest content of protein. Having 3 servings of this food means fulfilling 25% of required protein in average. To make it 100%, vegetables especially beans are the main source of protein where in 100 grams of beans it contains 42% of protein needed for average human body. A cup of milk also contributes to 16% of protein required and almost 20% of B12.

Talking about milk, it is one of the highest source of calcium for healthy teeth, 3 cups of milk a day are more than enough to meet daily recommendation of calcium. However, if 3 cups are too much, other foods like beans and other vegetables famous in India are capable to fill the gaps. Karela, an Indian food made of bitter melon has high content of vitamin A, 100 grams of it contains almost 50% of vitamin A and almost 100% of vitamin C needed by our body. Bitter melon also has high amount of vitamin B6 (40%) followed by beans (25%). Indian foods themselves do not contain much sugar, almost all food source has below 10% of recommended sugar intake. The highest sugar found is in milk (43% per cup of milk).
