Italy

Average daily Italian nutrition intake has high to very high amount of sugar, calcium, vitamin B12, C, B6, and protein. This is due to the fact, people of Italian background consume a lot of animal products, fruit, and vegetables mainly rich in phosphate, protein, and vitamin B6. However, there is a need to cut sugar intake as average Italians take double of recommended sugar amount daily. The high sugar amount in Italian diet habit comes naturally from vegetables, fruit, and milk. They also need increase vitamin A intake to satisfy recommended daily value.

Food source rich in:
  • Vitamin A: butter, cheese, egg, sweet potato, squash, carrot

Italians start the day simply with a croissant or slice of brioche, accompanied by a cappuccino, or just an espresso. Lunch usually consists of a three-course meal: appetizer, first plate which is usually pasta, soup or rice, followed by the second plate of meat or fish dish; a leisurely affair ending with fresh fruit. Italians prefer to enjoy sweet cakes and pastries with a mid-afternoon coffee break.

There are various Italian foods known worldwide and it hard to view the habit from certain food. However, pasta is the most basic calorie for most Italians. Pasta alone does not have much nutritional value; however, its sauce and fillings are. One of the famous sauce for pasta is tomato-based sauce. Four cups of tomato contain enough vitamin A and C for the day. Marinara sauce which includes fish and shrimp also rich in nutrition to maintain healthy teeth. 300 grams of fish or prawn are enough to maintain recommended intake of protein, phosphorus, sugar, vitamin B6 and C. Pork also sometimes used in pasta, and it has comparable amount of nutrition as fish and prawn. The way they cook the pasta also enrich the nutrition, eggs and milk are not strange to be added into the pasta. Fruits they usually have after meal are also good source of vitamins and minerals good for teeth.

There is no doubt Italian food are rich in flavour as well as nutritional value. In a daily habit, Italian has enough nutrition to maintain healthy teeth. The fact that, they may have “overdose” in a good way with beneficial nutrition to keep the teeth from decaying. However, natural sugar from milk and fruit as well as sugar added into cooking must be taken into consideration. Too much sugar is very bad and lead to teeth’s deterioration. Although, Italian food are basically good, it does not mean, one can eat all the food at once in a day. A precaution of excessive nutrition taken in a day may not be good for overall bodily health.
